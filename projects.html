01

Battery Discharge Station

Overview

Designed and built a high-power battery discharge station for safely cycling lithium-ion battery packs. The system supports variable load profiles, real-time telemetry, and automated shutdown on fault conditions.

Design Highlights

Implemented precharge protection circuitry to limit inrush current on connection, and integrated remote monitoring via serial communication. An emergency shutdown relay triggers on overcurrent, overvoltage, or thermal events.

Outcome

Successfully discharged and characterized multiple high-capacity battery packs, providing reliable capacity and internal resistance data.

02

Controls Design

Overview

Developed and compared PID and LQR control strategies for an inverted pendulum system, validating performance through both simulation and hardware experimentation.

Approach

Derived linearized state-space models and solved the Riccati equation numerically to compute optimal LQR gains. Simulated closed-loop response in Julia before deploying to physical hardware.

Results

LQR outperformed hand-tuned PID in settling time and robustness to disturbances, with quantitative comparisons documented in a written analysis report.

03

Manufacturing Project

Overview

Designed custom mechanical components from concept through fabrication, using a combination of CNC machining and additive manufacturing to produce functional hardware.

Process

Parts were modeled in SolidWorks, tolerance-analyzed for fit, and manufactured using a 3-axis CNC mill and FDM 3D printing. Post-processing included surface finishing and assembly verification.

Skills Applied

GD&T for design drawings, CAM toolpath generation, and hands-on operation of machining equipment. Iterated on designs based on physical test fit results.
